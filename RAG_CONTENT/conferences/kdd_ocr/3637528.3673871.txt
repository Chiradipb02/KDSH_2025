Abstract
This paper provides an overview of the ACM SIGKDD 2024 AI4Science4AI
special day. It includes information about the organizers, invited
speakers, keynote speakers, the event agenda, and insights from
related workshops. The AI4Science4AI special day aims to bring
together experts in artificial intelligence (AI) and science to discuss
the latest developments, challenges, and future directions.

1 Introduction
The ACM SIGKDD 2024 AI4Science4AI special day is a pioneering
event dedicated to exploring the dynamic intersection of artificial
intelligence (AI) and scientific research. This groundbreaking gath-
ering aims to create a vibrant platform where leading researchers
and practitioners from diverse fields can converge to share their
cutting-edge findings, tackle pressing challenges, and envision the
future of AI-driven scientific discovery. By fostering an environ-
ment of collaboration and innovation, this special day seeks to
accelerate advancements in AI applications across scientific disci-
plines, ultimately pushing the boundaries of what is possible in
both AI technology and scientific inquiry.

3 Invited Speakers and Panelists
The AI4Science4AI special day will feature an exceptional lineup of
invited speakers and panelists from across the globe, representing
diverse disciplines from government, industry, and academia. These
experts are at the forefront of AI and scientific research, driving
innovation and making groundbreaking contributions in their re-
spective fields. Below is the list of confirmed invited speakers and
their affiliations:
•Yiu-ming Cheung: Chair Professor, Hong Kong Baptist
University, Hong Kong
•Nikunj Oza: Leader, Data Sciences Group, NASA, USA
•Hong Yu: Professor, founding Director of Center for Biomed-
ical and Health Research in Data Sciences, University of
Massachusetts at Lowell, USA
Yiu-ming Cheung is a Chair Professor at the Department of Com-
puter Science at Hong Kong Baptist University. He is also a Fellow
of IEEE, BCS, the American Association for the Advancement of
Science (AAAS), and the Institution of Engineering and Technology
(IET). His research interests include machine learning and visual
computing and their applications in data science, pattern recogni-
tion, multi-objective optimization, and information security. Prof.
Cheung has significantly contributed to developing robust learning
algorithms and their applications in various domains. He has pub-
lished over 300 research papers in top-tier journals and conferences
and has served as an editor and reviewer for numerous prestigious
journals. Prof. Cheung has received several awards for his research
excellence, including the RGC Senior Research Fellow Award and
the 2023 APNNS Outstanding Achievement Award.
Nikunj Oza is the leader of the Data Sciences Group at NASA
Ames Research Center. He specializes in developing and applying
machine learning and data mining techniques to aerospace applica-
tions, including aviation safety, system health management, and
climate science. Dr. Oza has been instrumental in advancing AI
and machine learning within NASA to improve the safety and ef-
ficiency of aviation systems and space missions. He has authored

5 Insights from Related Workshops
Insights from related NSF workshops highlight the importance of
overcoming challenges and leveraging AI advances in scientific
research. The key points will be discussed on the special day.
•NSF Sponsored Workshop on AI-Enabled Scientific
Revolution: Highlighted the need to address gaps such as
limited labeled data, robustness, bias, causality, uncertainty
quantification, and explainability [2].
•NSF Meta-Workshop: AI to Accelerate Science and En-
gineering Discovery (AI2ASED): Focused on leveraging
scientific knowledge, self-supervised learning, transfer learn-
ing, generative models, trustworthy AI, and intelligent au-
tomation [1].
5.1 Key Challenges and Barriers
•Limited labeled data in scientific domains.
•Robustness and safety concerns due to out-of-distribution
data and adversarial attacks.
•Bias and fairness in AI models.•Understanding causality in scientific research.
•Quantifying uncertainty in AI predictions.
•Explainability of AI models.
•Data sharing and computational costs.
5.2 Potential AI Advances
•Incorporating scientific knowledge into AI models.
•Leveraging data from scientific simulations.
•Self-supervised, semi-supervised, and transfer learning.
•Generative foundation models with reasoning capabilities.
•Trustworthy AI and understanding causality.
•Beyond predictive machine learning to prescriptive model-
ing.
•Intelligent automation of research workflows.
•Next-generation data collection, curation, and sharing.
•Shared cyberinfrastructure for AI.
5.3 Recommendations
•Incentivizing cross-disciplinary AI research.
•Developing science-driven AI research ecosystems.
•Looking beyond standard performance metrics.
•Supporting sustained transdisciplinary collaborations.
•Addressing ethics in AI-enabled science.
•Promoting transdisciplinary training.
